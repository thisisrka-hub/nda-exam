# NDA 2026 English Vocabulary Notes

## Synonyms
Synonyms are words that have similar meanings. They help in understanding context and improving vocabulary.
- **Examples**: Happy - Joyful, Sad - Unhappy, Big - Large.
- **Tips**: Look for words that can replace each other without changing the meaning. Common in exams: Abundant - Plentiful, Brave - Courageous.

## Antonyms
Antonyms are words with opposite meanings. Useful for contrast and precision.
- **Examples**: Hot - Cold, Fast - Slow, Rich - Poor.
- **Tips**: Prefixes like un-, in-, dis- often indicate opposites. E.g., Honest - Dishonest.

## Idioms and Phrases
Idioms are expressions with figurative meanings. Phrases are groups of words with specific meanings.
- **Examples**: "Kick the bucket" (die), "Break a leg" (good luck), "Piece of cake" (easy).
- **Tips**: Learn common ones; they appear in fill-in-the-blanks or meaning questions.

## One-Word Substitution
Replace a phrase with a single word.
- **Examples**: One who studies rocks - Geologist, Fear of heights - Acrophobia.
- **Tips**: Memorize common substitutions for professions, qualities, etc.

## Choosing Appropriate Words for Blanks
Fill in blanks with words that fit contextually.
- **Tips**: Consider grammar, meaning, and flow. Synonyms/antonyms often used.
