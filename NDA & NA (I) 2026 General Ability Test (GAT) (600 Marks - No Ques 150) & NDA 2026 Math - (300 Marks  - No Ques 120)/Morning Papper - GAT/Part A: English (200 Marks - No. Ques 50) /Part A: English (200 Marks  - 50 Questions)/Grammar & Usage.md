# NDA 2026 English Grammar & Usage Notes

## Spotting Errors
- **Common Errors**: Subject-verb agreement, pronoun errors, tense inconsistencies, preposition misuse, article errors.
- **Tips**: Read the sentence carefully; check for grammatical rules; look for redundancy or awkward phrasing.
- **Examples**:
  - Incorrect: He go to school. → Correct: He goes to school. (Subject-verb agreement)
  - Incorrect: She is taller than me. → Correct: She is taller than I. (Pronoun case)

## Sentence Improvement
- **Focus**: Replace awkward or incorrect parts with better alternatives.
- **Tips**: Choose the option that makes the sentence clearer, more concise, and grammatically correct.
- **Examples**:
  - Original: He is too tired that he cannot work. → Improved: He is so tired that he cannot work.
  - Original: The book was read by me yesterday. → Improved: I read the book yesterday. (Prefer active voice)

## Sentence Correction
- **Types**: Correct grammar, punctuation, structure.
- **Tips**: Identify the error type; ensure logical flow.
- **Examples**:
  - Incorrect: Walking down the street, the rain started. → Correct: While walking down the street, it started raining. (Dangling modifier)
  - Incorrect: Neither he nor his friends is going. → Correct: Neither he nor his friends are going. (Agreement with nearest subject)

## Tenses
- **Present Simple**: Facts, habits (e.g., I eat breakfast).
- **Present Continuous**: Ongoing actions (e.g., I am eating).
- **Past Simple**: Completed actions (e.g., I ate).
- **Past Continuous**: Ongoing in past (e.g., I was eating).
- **Present Perfect**: Actions with present relevance (e.g., I have eaten).
- **Past Perfect**: Earlier past actions (e.g., I had eaten).
- **Future Simple**: Future actions (e.g., I will eat).
- **Tips**: Match tense to time frame; avoid mixing tenses unnecessarily.

## Prepositions
- **Common Prepositions**: In, on, at, for, with, by, to, from.
- **Usage**:
  - Time: At 5 PM, on Monday, in 2023.
  - Place: At home, on the table, in India.
  - Other: Interested in, depend on, agree with.
- **Tips**: Prepositions are idiomatic; learn common phrases (e.g., good at, bad in).

## Active and Passive Voice
- **Active**: Subject performs action (e.g., The dog bit the man).
- **Passive**: Subject receives action (e.g., The man was bitten by the dog).
- **Conversion**: Active to Passive: Object becomes subject; add "by" + original subject; change verb to be + past participle.
- **Tips**: Use passive for unknown doer or formal writing; active for direct, energetic sentences.

## Direct and Indirect Speech
- **Direct Speech**: Quotes exact words (e.g., He said, "I am tired.").
- **Indirect Speech**: Reports words (e.g., He said that he was tired.).
- **Rules**:
  - Change pronouns, tenses (present → past), time/place words (now → then, here → there).
  - Reporting verbs: Said, told, asked.
- **Tips**: For questions, use "asked if/whether"; for commands, use "told/ordered to".

## General Tips for NDA English
- Practice daily with mock tests.
- Focus on error-prone areas like subject-verb agreement and prepositions.
- Read newspapers and books to improve usage.
- Time management: Allocate 1-2 minutes per question.
