# NDA 2026 Physics: Physical Properties and States of Matter (500 MCQs)

## Important Notes and Formulas

### States of Matter and Physical Properties
- Matter can exist as solid, liquid, or gas. Solids have fixed shape and volume, liquids have fixed volume but variable shape, and gases have neither fixed shape nor fixed volume.
- Physical properties include mass, weight, volume, density, specific gravity, boiling point, melting point, color, and refractive index.
- Intensive properties do not depend on the amount of substance: density, temperature, pressure, specific gravity.
- Extensive properties depend on amount of substance: mass, volume, weight, energy.

### Mass, Weight, Volume, Density, Specific Gravity
- Mass is amount of matter, measured in kilograms (kg). It is independent of gravity.
- Weight is the gravitational force on a mass: W = mg, measured in newtons (N).
- Volume is the space occupied by an object, measured in cubic metres (m^3), litres (L), or cm^3.
- Density is mass per unit volume: ρ = m/V.
- Specific gravity is the ratio of density of a substance to density of water at 4°C: S.G. = ρ_substance / ρ_water.
- Specific gravity is dimensionless.
- If S.G. < 1, the object floats on water; if S.G. > 1, it sinks.

### Principle of Archimedes
- A body immersed in a fluid experiences an upward buoyant force equal to the weight of fluid displaced.
- Buoyant force F_b = ρ_fluid V_displaced g.
- Apparent weight in fluid W_app = W - F_b.
- For a floating object, weight of object = weight of displaced fluid.
- Fraction submerged for a floating object = density_object / density_fluid.

### Pressure and Barometer
- Pressure P = F/A. SI unit is pascal (Pa) = N/m^2.
- Fluid pressure at depth h is P = P_0 + ρ g h.
- Atmospheric pressure at sea level is about 1.013 × 10^5 Pa or 760 mm Hg.
- A mercury barometer measures atmospheric pressure with a column of mercury: P_atm = ρ_Hg g h.
- In a manometer, pressure difference is measured using fluid height difference.

### Key Formulas
- W = mg.
- ρ = m/V.
- S.G. = ρ_substance / ρ_water.
- F_b = ρ_fluid V_displaced g.
- W_app = W - F_b.
- P = F/A.
- P = P_0 + ρ g h.
- h = P_atm / (ρ g) for barometer fluid column.
