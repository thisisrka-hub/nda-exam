# NDA 2026 Physics: Static and Current Electricity (500 MCQs)

## Important Notes and Formulas

### Static Electricity
- Static electricity is the accumulation of electric charge on the surface of an object.
- Like charges repel and unlike charges attract.
- Electrostatic force between point charges is given by Coulomb's law: F = k q1 q2 / r^2 where k = 9 × 10^9 N m^2/C^2.
- Charge is measured in coulombs (C). 1 electron charge = 1.6 × 10^-19 C.
- Conductors allow charges to move freely; nonconductors (insulators) do not.
- Charging by friction, conduction, and induction are the three common methods.
- A charged conductor has charge only on its outer surface.
- Electric field lines start on positive charges and end on negative charges.

### Conductors and Nonconductors
- Conductors: materials like copper, aluminum, silver, gold, and graphite that allow current to flow easily.
- Nonconductors (insulators): materials like rubber, glass, wood, plastic that do not allow free electron flow.
- Semiconductors have conductivity between conductors and insulators.
- In conductors, free electrons move under an electric field.
- In insulators, charges remain bound to atoms and do not move freely.

### Ohm's Law
- Ohm's law states that V = IR for many conductors, where V is voltage, I is current, and R is resistance.
- Resistance R = ρ L/A, where ρ is resistivity, L is length, and A is cross-sectional area.
- The unit of resistance is ohm (Ω). 1 Ω = 1 V/A.
- Resistivity depends on material and temperature.
- Conductance G = 1/R, measured in siemens (S).

### Simple Electrical Circuits
- Current is the rate of flow of charge: I = Q/t.
- Circuit elements include battery, resistor, switch, wire, and lamp.
- Series circuit: same current flows through all components; total resistance R_total = R1 + R2 + ... .
- Parallel circuit: same voltage across each branch; total conductance G_total = G1 + G2 + ..., or 1/R_total = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + ... .
- Kirchhoff's laws: junction rule (sum of currents at node = 0) and loop rule (sum of voltage changes around loop = 0).
- Electric power P = VI = I^2 R = V^2 / R.
- Energy consumed E = Pt, measured in joules or kilowatt-hours.

### Heating, Lighting and Magnetic Effects of Current
- Heating effect of current: electric current through a resistor produces heat. Heat produced H = I^2 R t (Joule's law).
- Incandescent lamps produce light by heating a filament; fluorescent lamps use gas discharge.
- Magnetic effect of current: a current-carrying conductor produces a magnetic field around it.
- Right-hand rule for straight conductor: thumb in current direction, curled fingers show magnetic field direction.
- A current in a coil produces a magnetic field like a bar magnet; such a coil is called a solenoid.
- Force on a current-carrying wire in a magnetic field: F = BIL sin θ.
- Electric bell, relay, and electromagnet use magnetic effects of current.

### Key Formulas
- Coulomb's law: F = k q1 q2 / r^2.
- Electric field E = F/q.
- Voltage V = W/Q.
- Current I = Q/t.
- Ohm's law: V = IR.
- Resistance: R = ρ L/A.
- Power: P = VI = I^2 R = V^2 / R.
- Energy: E = Pt.
- Heat: H = I^2 R t.
- Magnetic force: F = B I L sin θ.
