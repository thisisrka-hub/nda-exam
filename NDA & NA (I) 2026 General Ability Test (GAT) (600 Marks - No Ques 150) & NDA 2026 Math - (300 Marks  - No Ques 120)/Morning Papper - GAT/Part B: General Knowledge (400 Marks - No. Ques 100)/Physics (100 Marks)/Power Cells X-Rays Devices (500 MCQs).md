# NDA 2026 Physics: Electrical Power, Cells, X-Rays, and Devices (500 MCQs)

## Important Notes and Formulas

### Measurement of Electrical Power
- Electrical power is the rate at which electrical energy is consumed or produced.
- Power P = VI, where V is voltage and I is current.
- Equivalent forms: P = I^2 R and P = V^2 / R.
- Energy consumed E = Pt, measured in joules (J) or kilowatt-hours (kWh).
- One kilowatt-hour = 3.6 × 10^6 J.

### Primary and Secondary Cells
- Primary cells are non-rechargeable cells, e.g. dry cell, alkaline cell.
- Secondary cells are rechargeable, e.g. lead-acid battery, nickel-cadmium battery.
- EMF of a cell is the voltage it can provide when no current flows.
- Internal resistance of a cell reduces terminal voltage under load: V_terminal = EMF - I r.
- Capacity of a cell is the total charge it can deliver, measured in ampere-hours (Ah).

### Use of X-Rays
- X-rays are high-energy electromagnetic waves with short wavelength.
- They are produced by accelerating electrons and striking a metal target.
- X-rays are used in medical imaging, material inspection, and crystallography.
- X-rays are absorbed more by dense materials like bone than by soft tissue.
- X-ray exposure can be harmful, so shielding and minimum exposure are essential.

### Simple Pendulum
- A simple pendulum consists of a small bob suspended by a light string.
- Time period T = 2π √(L/g) for small oscillations.
- Frequency f = 1/T.
- Time period is independent of mass and amplitude (for small angles).

### Simple Pulleys
- A single movable pulley reduces the effort force by a factor of 2.
- Mechanical advantage = load / effort.
- A single fixed pulley changes the direction of force but not its magnitude.
- Ideal pulley assumes no friction and massless rope.

### Siphon
- A siphon transfers liquid from higher to lower container using atmospheric pressure and gravity.
- Liquid flows over a height greater than the source surface if the tube is filled and outlet is lower.

### Levers
- Levers have three classes depending on the relative positions of load, effort, and pivot.
- Mechanical advantage = effort arm / load arm.
- A lever gives a force advantage at the expense of distance moved.

### Balloon
- Hot air balloons rise because heated air inside is less dense than outside air.
- Buoyant force from displaced air lifts the balloon.

### Pumps
- Pumps move fluids from one place to another using mechanical action.
- A suction pump draws liquid into a cylinder and lifts it when the piston moves.
- Centrifugal pumps use rotation to move liquid outward by centrifugal force.

### Hydrometer
- A hydrometer measures the specific gravity of liquids by floating at a level depending on density.
- A hydrometer floats higher in denser liquids.

### Pressure Cooker
- A pressure cooker increases pressure inside, raising boiling point and cooking food faster.
- Pressure inside is P = P_atm + ρ g h in the vapor phase, but the practical principle is sealed steam pressure.

### Thermos Flask
- A thermos flask reduces heat transfer by conduction, convection, and radiation.
- It has a vacuum between inner and outer walls, silvered surfaces, and insulating stoppers.

### Gramophone
- A gramophone converts vibration of a stylus in a groove into sound using a diaphragm and horn.

### Telegraphs
- Telegraphs transmit messages as electrical signals over wires using Morse code.
- A key completes and breaks a circuit to send dots and dashes.

### Telephone
- A telephone converts sound to electrical signals and back to sound through a transmitter and receiver.

### Periscope
- A periscope uses two mirrors at 45° to allow observation from a hidden position.

### Telescope
- A telescope uses lenses or mirrors to gather light and form distant images.
- Angular magnification depends on focal lengths of objective and eyepiece.

### Microscope
- A microscope uses objective and eyepiece lenses to magnify small objects.
- Total magnification = magnification of objective × magnification of eyepiece.

### Mariner’s Compass
- A mariner’s compass is a magnetic compass used for navigation at sea.
- It points to magnetic north and is mounted to rotate freely.

### Lightning Conductors
- A lightning conductor provides a safe path for lightning to ground.
- It consists of a pointed conductor connected to earth by a thick cable.

### Safety Fuses
- A fuse is a safety device that melts when current exceeds a safe limit.
- It protects circuits from overload and short circuits.
