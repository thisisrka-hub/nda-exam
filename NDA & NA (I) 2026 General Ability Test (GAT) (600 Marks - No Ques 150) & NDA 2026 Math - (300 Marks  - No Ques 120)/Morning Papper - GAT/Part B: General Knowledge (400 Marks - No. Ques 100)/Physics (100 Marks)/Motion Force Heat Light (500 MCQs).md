# NDA 2026 Physics: Motion, Forces, Heat, Light (500 MCQs)

## Important Notes and Formulas

### Motion of Objects
- Motion is the change of position of an object with time.
- Distance is the total path length traveled. Displacement is the straight-line change in position.
- Speed = distance/time. Velocity = displacement/time. Velocity is a vector and may be positive or negative.
- Uniform motion: constant speed. Non-uniform motion: varying speed.
- Average speed = total distance/total time. Average velocity = total displacement/total time.
- Acceleration = change in velocity/time. Units: m/s^2.
- Constant acceleration equations: v = u + at, s = ut + 1/2 at^2, v^2 = u^2 + 2as.

### Newton's Laws of Motion
- 1st law: A body remains at rest or uniform motion unless acted on by a net force.
- 2nd law: Net force F = ma. Force and acceleration are directly proportional.
- 3rd law: For every action there is an equal and opposite reaction.
- Inertia is the tendency to resist change in motion. Mass measures inertia.
- Friction opposes relative motion between surfaces.

### Force and Momentum
- Momentum p = mv. It is conserved in closed systems.
- Impulse J = change in momentum = F Δt.
- Conservation of linear momentum: total momentum before = total momentum after in collisions.
- Elastic collision: kinetic energy conserved. Inelastic collision: kinetic energy not conserved.

### Parallelogram of Forces
- If two forces acting at a point are represented by two adjacent sides of a parallelogram, the diagonal gives the resultant.
- Resultant R = √(P^2 + Q^2 + 2PQ cos θ) for forces P and Q with angle θ between them.
- When forces are perpendicular: R = √(P^2 + Q^2).
- Equilibrium of a body: resultant force = 0 and resultant moment = 0.

### Stability and Equilibrium
- Stable equilibrium: object returns to its original position after a small disturbance.
- Unstable equilibrium: object moves further away after a disturbance.
- Neutral equilibrium: object remains in its new position after disturbance.
- Centre of gravity and base area determine stability.
- A body is in equilibrium if the sum of forces in every direction is zero.

### Gravitation
- Newton’s law of gravitation: F = G m1 m2 / r^2.
- Gravitational constant G = 6.67 × 10^-11 N m^2 / kg^2.
- Gravitational field strength g = GM / R^2 for Earth.
- Weight W = mg. Mass is constant, weight varies with gravity.
- Acceleration due to gravity near Earth’s surface ≈ 9.8 m/s^2.

### Work, Power, and Energy
- Work done W = F s cos θ when force F moves object through displacement s.
- SI unit of work and energy is joule (J).
- Kinetic energy KE = 1/2 mv^2.
- Potential energy PE = mgh for near Earth's surface.
- Work-energy theorem: net work done = change in kinetic energy.
- Power = work done/time. Unit watt (W) = J/s.
- Mechanical energy is sum of kinetic and potential energies.

### Effects of Heat
- Heat is energy transfer due to temperature difference.
- Temperature is measured with mercury, alcohol, or thermocouples.
- Celsius, Fahrenheit, Kelvin are temperature scales. T(K) = T(°C) + 273.
- Specific heat capacity c: heat required to raise 1 kg of substance by 1°C.
- Heat transfer Q = mcΔT.
- Latent heat L is heat required for change of state without temperature change.
- Heat for phase change: Q = mL.
- Modes of heat transfer: conduction, convection, radiation.
- Conduction is heat transfer through direct contact; good in solids.
- Convection is heat transfer by fluid motion.
- Radiation is heat transfer by electromagnetic waves; no medium needed.

### Sound Waves and Musical Instruments
- Sound is a longitudinal wave produced by vibrating objects.
- Frequency determines pitch; amplitude determines loudness.
- Speed of sound in air ≈ 343 m/s at 20°C.
- Wavelength λ, frequency f, and speed v are related by v = fλ.
- Echo is reflection of sound.
- Simple musical instruments include strings, wind pipes, and percussion.
- The human ear hears sound between about 20 Hz and 20 kHz.

### Light: Reflection and Refraction
- Light travels in straight lines in a uniform medium (rectilinear propagation).
- Angle of incidence equals angle of reflection.
- Refraction: bending of light when it passes from one medium to another.
- Snell’s law: n1 sin i = n2 sin r.
- Refractive index n = sin i / sin r = c/v.
- Total internal reflection occurs when light goes from denser to rarer medium and angle of incidence exceeds critical angle.

### Spherical Mirrors and Lenses
- Concave mirror: converging mirror. Convex mirror: diverging mirror.
- Mirror formula: 1/f = 1/v + 1/u. Magnification m = h_i / h_o = v / u.
- Lens formula: 1/f = 1/v - 1/u for thin lenses in standard sign convention.
- Convex lens is converging, concave lens is diverging.
- Image formation depends on object position relative to focal length.

### Human Eye
- The eye forms images on the retina.
- The eye lens changes focal length via accommodation.
- Near point of normal eye is 25 cm. Far point is infinity for a normal eye.
- Common defects: myopia (short-sightedness), hypermetropia (long-sightedness), corrected by concave or convex lenses.
