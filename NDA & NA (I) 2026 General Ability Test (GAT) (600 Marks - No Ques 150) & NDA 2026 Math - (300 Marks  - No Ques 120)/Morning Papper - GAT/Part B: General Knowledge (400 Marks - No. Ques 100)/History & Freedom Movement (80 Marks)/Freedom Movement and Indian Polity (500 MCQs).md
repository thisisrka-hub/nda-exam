# Freedom Movement and Indian Polity (500 MCQs)

## Important Notes

### Freedom Movement in India
- **1857 Sepoy Mutiny**: First war of independence, causes (Doctrine of Lapse, greased cartridges), leaders (Bahadur Shah Zafar, Rani Lakshmibai), consequences (end of East India Company rule).
- **Moderates and Extremists**: Moderates (Dadabhai Naoroji, Gopal Krishna Gokhale) sought reforms; Extremists (Bal Gangadhar Tilak, Lala Lajpat Rai) demanded Swaraj.
- **Partition of Bengal (1905)**: Curzon's divide-and-rule policy; Swadeshi Movement (boycott of British goods), Surat Split (1907).
- **Home Rule Movement (1915-1918)**: Annie Besant and Tilak demanded self-government.
- **Non-Cooperation Movement (1920-1922)**: Gandhi's response to Jallianwala Bagh Massacre; boycott of titles, courts, schools.
- **Civil Disobedience Movement (1930-1934)**: Salt March (Dandi), Durgabai Deshmukh; Poona Pact (1932).
- **Quit India Movement (1942)**: 'Do or Die' call; underground activities, Rani Gaidinliu.
- **Key Figures**: Gandhi (non-violent leader), Nehru (socialist ideals), Sardar Patel (unification), Subhas Chandra Bose (Azad Hind Fauj).

### Elementary Study of Indian Constitution and Administration
- **Preamble**: Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic Republic; Justice, Liberty, Equality, Fraternity.
- **Fundamental Rights**: Right to Equality, Freedom, against Exploitation, Freedom of Religion, Cultural Rights, Right to Education, Right to Constitutional Remedies.
- **Directive Principles**: Guidelines for state policy, including welfare, education, environment.
- **Parliament**: Lok Sabha (House of People), Rajya Sabha (Council of States); President (nominal head), Prime Minister (real executive).
- **Judiciary**: Supreme Court (Chief Justice), High Courts; Independent judiciary.
- **Federal Structure**: Centre-State relations, Finance Commission, All India Services.

### Elementary Knowledge of Five Year Plans of India
- **First Plan (1951-56)**: Agriculture focus, Damodar Valley Project.
- **Second Plan (1956-61)**: Industrialization, public sector expansion.
- **Third Plan (1961-66)**: Self-sufficiency, Green Revolution.
- **Fourth Plan (1969-74)**: Growth with stability, family planning.
- **Fifth Plan (1974-79)**: Poverty eradication, minimum needs.
- **Sixth Plan (1980-85)**: Economic liberalization beginnings.
- **Seventh Plan (1985-90)**: Food security, employment.
- **Eighth Plan (1992-97)**: Liberalization, privatization.
- **Ninth Plan (1997-2002)**: Human development.
- **Tenth Plan (2002-07)**: Inclusive growth.
- **Eleventh Plan (2007-12)**: Faster growth, sustainable development.
- **Twelfth Plan (2012-17)**: Inclusive and sustainable growth.

### Panchayati Raj, Co-operatives and Community Development
- **Panchayati Raj**: 73rd Amendment (1992), three-tier system: Gram Panchayat, Panchayat Samiti (Block), Zila Parishad (District).
- **Powers**: Gram Sabha, reservation for SC/ST/Women.
- **Co-operatives**: Types - Consumer, Producer, Credit; Role in rural economy, Amul example.
- **Community Development**: Post-independence programs for rural upliftment, National Extension Service (1953).

### Bhoodan, Sarvodaya, National Integration and Welfare State
- **Bhoodan**: Vinoba Bhave's movement (1951), voluntary land donation to landless.
- **Sarvodaya**: Gandhi's philosophy of universal upliftment, Gram Swaraj.
- **National Integration**: Unity in diversity, threats (communalism, regionalism), measures (National Integration Council).
- **Welfare State**: Government's responsibility for citizens' welfare, social security, public services.

### Basic Teachings of Mahatma Gandhi
- **Non-violence (Ahimsa)**: Core principle, means and end.
- **Truth (Satya)**: Soul force, Satyagraha.
- **Civil Disobedience**: Defying unjust laws peacefully.
- **Swaraj**: Self-rule, economic independence.
- **Swadeshi**: Boycott foreign goods, promote local.
- **Religious Harmony**: Tolerance, Ram Rajya.
- **Removal of Untouchability**: Social equality, Harijan upliftment.
