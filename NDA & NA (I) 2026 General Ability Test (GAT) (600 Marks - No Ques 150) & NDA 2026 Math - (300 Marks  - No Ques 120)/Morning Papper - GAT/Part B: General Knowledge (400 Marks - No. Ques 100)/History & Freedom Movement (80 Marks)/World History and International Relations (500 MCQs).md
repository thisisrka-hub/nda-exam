# World History and International Relations (500 MCQs)

## Important Notes

### Forces Shaping the Modern World
- **Globalization**: Interconnected economies, cultures, and societies through trade, technology, and migration.
- **Nationalism**: Rise of nation-states, self-determination movements, e.g., unification of Germany and Italy.
- **Imperialism**: European powers colonizing Africa, Asia; scramble for colonies in 19th century.
- **Industrialization**: Shift from agrarian to industrial societies, urbanization, capitalism.
- **Ideological Conflicts**: Cold War between capitalism and communism, proxy wars.
- **Technological Advancements**: Internet, nuclear power, space exploration influencing global dynamics.

### Renaissance, Exploration and Discovery
- **Renaissance (14th-17th century)**: Rebirth of art, literature, science in Europe; humanism, figures like Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, Galileo.
- **Exploration and Discovery**: Age of Discovery (15th-17th century); Columbus (1492, Americas), Vasco da Gama (1498, sea route to India), Magellan (circumnavigation).
- **Motives**: Search for new trade routes, gold, spices; spread of Christianity.
- **Consequences**: Columbian Exchange (transfer of plants, animals, diseases), European dominance.

### War of American Independence
- **Causes**: British taxation policies (Stamp Act, Tea Act), 'No taxation without representation'.
- **Key Events**: Boston Tea Party (1773), Declaration of Independence (1776), Yorktown (1781).
- **Leaders**: George Washington (commander), Thomas Jefferson (Declaration author).
- **Outcome**: Treaty of Paris (1783), birth of United States, inspiration for other revolutions.

### French Revolution, Industrial Revolution, and Russian Revolution
- **French Revolution (1789-1799)**: Overthrow of monarchy, 'Liberty, Equality, Fraternity'; Reign of Terror, rise of Napoleon.
- **Industrial Revolution (1760-1840)**: Began in Britain, steam engine (James Watt), factories, mechanization; spread to Europe, America.
- **Russian Revolution (1917)**: February Revolution (overthrew Tsar), October Revolution (Bolsheviks, Lenin); establishment of Soviet Union, communism.

### Impact of Science and Technology on Society
- **Positive Impacts**: Medical advancements (vaccines, surgery), communication (internet, mobile), transportation (airplanes, cars), education access.
- **Negative Impacts**: Environmental pollution, nuclear weapons, job displacement, privacy issues.
- **Examples**: Atomic bomb (Hiroshima), genetic engineering, AI revolution.

### Concept of One World, United Nations, Panchsheel, Democracy, Socialism and Communism
- **One World**: Global interdependence, 'global village' concept by Marshall McLuhan.
- **United Nations (1945)**: Founded after WWII; organs: General Assembly, Security Council, Secretariat; peacekeeping, SDGs.
- **Panchsheel**: Five principles of peaceful coexistence (1954, India-China): mutual respect, non-aggression, non-interference, equality, peaceful coexistence.
- **Democracy**: Government by the people, for the people; types: direct, representative.
- **Socialism**: Economic system with state ownership of means of production, social welfare.
- **Communism**: Classless, stateless society; Marxist ideology, implemented in USSR, China.

### Role of India in the Present World
- **Economic Power**: Fastest-growing major economy, IT hub, pharmaceuticals, space program (ISRO).
- **Diplomatic Role**: Non-aligned movement founder, BRICS member, G20, UN peacekeeping contributions.
- **Soft Power**: Yoga, Bollywood, democracy promotion, cultural exports.
- **Challenges**: Poverty, inequality, climate change; aspirations for UNSC permanent seat.
- **Global Partnerships**: Strategic ties with US, Russia, Japan; leadership in South-South cooperation.
