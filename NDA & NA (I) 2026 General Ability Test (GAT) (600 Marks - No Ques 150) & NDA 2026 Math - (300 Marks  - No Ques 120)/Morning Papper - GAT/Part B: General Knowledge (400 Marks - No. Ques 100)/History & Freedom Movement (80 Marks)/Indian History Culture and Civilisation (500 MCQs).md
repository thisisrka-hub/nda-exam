# Indian History: Culture and Civilisation (500 MCQs)

## Important Notes

### Ancient India
- **Indus Valley Civilization (3300-1300 BCE)**: Known for advanced urban planning, drainage systems, undeciphered script. Major sites: Harappa, Mohenjo-Daro. Culture included seals, pottery, and possible matriarchal society.
- **Vedic Period (1500-500 BCE)**: Vedic literature (Rigveda, etc.), development of Hinduism, caste system (Brahmins, Kshatriyas, Vaishyas, Shudras). Emphasis on rituals, sacrifices, and early philosophical thoughts.
- **Mauryan Empire (321-185 BCE)**: Founded by Chandragupta Maurya. Ashoka's reign marked spread of Buddhism, rock edicts promoting dharma. Cultural patronage and administrative systems.
- **Gupta Golden Age (320-550 CE)**: Period of cultural flourishing. Advancements in mathematics (zero concept), astronomy, medicine (Charaka, Sushruta). Sanskrit literature, art, and architecture.

### Medieval India
- **Delhi Sultanate (1206-1526)**: Islamic rulers introduced Persian culture, architecture (Qutub Minar, Red Fort). Synthesis of Hindu-Muslim cultures.
- **Mughal Empire (1526-1857)**: Akbar's religious tolerance, cultural synthesis (Din-i-Ilahi). Shah Jahan's architectural marvels (Taj Mahal, Red Fort). Miniature paintings, Urdu language development.

### Modern India
- **British Colonial Rule (1757-1947)**: Impact on Indian culture - introduction of Western education, railways, but also cultural suppression. Renaissance movements (Bengal Renaissance), social reforms.
- **Freedom Movement**: Gandhi's non-violent struggle, emphasis on cultural revival. Nehru's vision of modern India.

### Cultural Aspects
- **Religion**: Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, Sikhism, Islam, Christianity. Festivals like Diwali, Holi, Eid.
- **Art and Architecture**: Temple architecture (Dravidian, Nagara), Mughal monuments, cave paintings (Ajanta, Ellora).
- **Literature**: Sanskrit epics (Ramayana, Mahabharata), Kalidasa's works, Bhakti poetry (Kabir, Tulsidas), modern literature.
- **Music and Dance**: Classical music (Hindustani, Carnatic), dances (Bharatanatyam, Kathakali, Kathak).
- **Philosophy and Science**: Ancient texts on astronomy, mathematics, medicine.
