# NDA 2026 General Science: Living vs Nonliving, Cells, Tissues, Growth & Reproduction (1000 MCQs)

## Important Notes and Formulas

### Characteristics of Living Things
- **Organization**: Highly organized structure with cells as basic unit.
- **Metabolism**: Chemical reactions converting nutrients into energy and building materials.
- **Growth**: Increase in size and complexity through cell division and differentiation.
- **Reproduction**: Ability to produce offspring (asexual or sexual).
- **Response to stimulus**: React to environmental changes (light, heat, chemicals, touch).
- **Homeostasis**: Maintain stable internal environment despite external changes.
- **Movement**: Change in position or shape (not always visible macroscopically).
- **Adaptation**: Traits suited to environment evolved over generations.
- **Respiration**: Release of energy from food molecules (aerobic or anaerobic).
- **Excretion**: Removal of metabolic waste products.

### Characteristics of Nonliving Things
- **No organization**: May have structure but no cellular organization.
- **No metabolism**: No chemical conversion of nutrients.
- **No growth**: Property of matter but not biological growth (e.g., crystal growth).
- **No reproduction**: Cannot produce offspring or replicate.
- **No response**: No active response to stimuli (passive changes only).
- **No homeostasis**: No regulation of internal conditions.
- **Examples**: Rocks, water, crystals, metals, air, soil, machines.

### The Cell
- **Definition**: Basic unit of life; smallest living unit.
- **Cell theory**: All living organisms made of cells; cells from pre-existing cells.
- **Discovery**: Robert Hooke (1665) observed cells in cork tissue.
- **Types**: Prokaryotic (bacteria, archaea) and Eukaryotic (plants, animals, fungi).
- **Prokaryotic cells**: No nucleus, no membrane-bound organelles, smaller (1-10 μm).
- **Eukaryotic cells**: Nucleus with DNA, membrane-bound organelles, larger (10-100 μm).

### Cell Components

**Prokaryotic Cell:**
- Cell membrane (plasma membrane)
- Cytoplasm
- Nucleoid region (DNA, not enclosed)
- Ribosomes (70S)
- Cell wall (bacteria): peptidoglycan

**Eukaryotic Animal Cell:**
- Cell/Plasma membrane: selective permeability
- Nucleus: contains DNA, separated by nuclear envelope
- Mitochondria: ATP production (cellular respiration)
- Endoplasmic reticulum: rough (ribosomes, protein synthesis) and smooth (lipid synthesis)
- Golgi apparatus: protein packaging and modification
- Lysosomes: digestion of cellular waste
- Ribosomes: protein synthesis (80S)
- Centrioles: organize microtubules
- Cytoplasm: gel-like substance containing organelles

**Eukaryotic Plant Cell (in addition):**
- Chloroplasts: photosynthesis (contain chlorophyll)
- Cell wall: cellulose, provides rigidity
- Large vacuole: storage, maintains turgidity
- Plasmodesmata: connections between adjacent cells

### Protoplasm
- **Definition**: Living substance inside cell (nucleus + cytoplasm).
- **Composition**: Water (70%), proteins (20%), lipids (5%), carbohydrates (3%), minerals (2%).
- **Properties**: Colloidal, semi-fluid, capable of movement.
- **Functions**: Maintains metabolism, growth, reproduction, conducts life activities.
- **In plant cell**: Cytoplasm surrounding vacuole.

### Tissues
- **Definition**: Group of similar cells with common origin and function.
- **Types**: Four main tissue types in animals; meristematic and permanent in plants.

**Animal Tissues:**
1. **Epithelial tissue**: Covers body surface and lines organs (cuboidal, columnar, squamous).
2. **Connective tissue**: Binds and supports (bone, cartilage, ligaments, tendons, blood).
3. **Muscular tissue**: Movement (skeletal, cardiac, smooth muscle).
4. **Nervous tissue**: Transmits signals (neurons, glial cells).

**Plant Tissues:**
1. **Meristematic tissue**: Actively dividing cells at shoot tip (apical), lateral bud, root tip (growth).
2. **Permanent tissue**:
   - **Dermal tissue**: Epidermis (outer layer protection), waxy cuticle.
   - **Ground tissue**: Parenchyma (living, flexible), collenchyma (support), sclerenchyma (support, with lignin).
   - **Vascular tissue**: Xylem (water transport, dead at maturity), Phloem (nutrient transport, living at maturity).

### Growth in Plants
- **Definition**: Increase in size and complexity; continuous process (indeterminate).
- **Primary growth**: Increase in length (apical meristem at root tip, shoot tip).
- **Secondary growth**: Increase in thickness (lateral meristem, cambium layer).
- **Phases**: Cell division (mitosis in meristem) → Cell elongation → Cell differentiation.

### Growth in Animals
- **Definition**: Increase in size (determinate in most animals).
- **Mechanism**: Cell division (mitosis) and enlargement; mostly in early developmental stages.
- **Growth hormones**: Human growth hormone (HGH) promotes protein synthesis.
- **Factors**: Nutrition, hormones, genetics, environment.

### Reproduction in Plants
**Asexual Reproduction:**
- **Fission**: One parent divides into two (algae, bacteria).
- **Budding**: Small outgrowth develops into new organism (yeast, hydra).
- **Vegetative propagation**: From roots (sweet potato), stems (potato tubers), leaves (Bryophyllum).
- **Fragmentation**: Breaking into fragments (algae, starfish).
- **Spore production**: Lightweight reproductive cells (ferns, mosses).
- **Advantages**: Fast, requires one parent, genetically identical (clones).

**Sexual Reproduction:**
- **Flowers**: Reproductive structures (sepals, petals, stamens, carpels).
- **Male gamete**: Pollen (2 or 3 cells) produced in anther.
- **Female gamete**: Ovule (7-celled embryo sac) in ovary.
- **Pollination**: Transfer of pollen to stigma (wind, insect, water, animal pollination).
- **Fertilization**: Male nucleus fuses with female nucleus (double fertilization in plants).
- **Seed formation**: Develops from ovule after fertilization.
- **Fruit formation**: Develops from ovary wall; protects and disperses seeds.

### Reproduction in Animals
**Asexual Reproduction (rare in animals):**
- **Budding**: Freshwater hydra produces buds.
- **Fission**: Starfish fragmentation, planaria regeneration.
- **Parthenogenesis**: Development from unfertilized egg (some insects, amphibians).

**Sexual Reproduction (most animals):**
- **Male reproductive system**: Produces sperm (gametes).
- **Female reproductive system**: Produces eggs/ova (gametes).
- **Fertilization**: Sperm fuses with egg forming zygote.
- **Development**: Zygote undergoes mitosis forming embryo (in womb or egg).
- **Gestation period**: Time from fertilization to birth (humans: 280 days).
- **Types of development**: 
  - **Oviparous**: Lay eggs (birds, reptiles, fish).
  - **Viviparous**: Live birth (mammals, some fish).
  - **Ovoviviparous**: Eggs hatch internally then born (some snakes).

### Cell Division
**Mitosis: Growth and Repair**
- **Phases**: Prophase → Metaphase → Anaphase → Telophase.
- **Result**: Two identical daughter cells (2n → 2n).
- **Purpose**: Growth, repair, replacement of cells.

**Meiosis: Sexual Reproduction**
- **Reduces chromosome number**: Two divisions (Meiosis I and II).
- **Result**: Four haploid cells (2n → n) → Gametes.
- **Crossing over**: Exchange of genetic material in Prophase I (genetic variation).
