# NDA 2026 General Science: Human Body, Vital Organs & Common Epidemics (1000 MCQs)

## Important Notes and Formulas

### Human Body Systems

**Skeletal System:**
- 206 bones in adult human (270 at birth, fused during growth).
- Functions: Support, protection, movement, mineral storage, blood cell production.
- Major bones: Femur (thighbone, longest), Humerus (arm), Ribs (12 pairs), Vertebral column (33 vertebrae).
- Joints: Movable (ball-and-socket, hinge, pivot), immovable (skull sutures).
- Bone composition: Calcium, phosphorus, collagen (flexibility).

**Muscular System:**
- 3 types: Skeletal (voluntary, striated), Cardiac (involuntary, striated), Smooth (involuntary, nonstriated).
- 600+ muscles in human body.
- Function: Movement, posture, heat production, support.
- Muscle action: Contraction (pulling) and relaxation; antagonistic pairs (biceps-triceps).

**Nervous System:**
- Central nervous system (CNS): Brain and spinal cord.
- Peripheral nervous system (PNS): Nerves connecting CNS to body.
- Brain: Cerebrum (thought, memory), Cerebellum (balance, coordination), Medulla (vital functions).
- Reflex arc: Stimulus to Sensory neuron to Spinal cord to Motor neuron to Response (no brain).

**Circulatory System:**
- Heart: 4 chambers (2 atria, 2 ventricles), pumps blood, ~72 bpm at rest.
- Blood: O2 transport by hemoglobin in RBCs, immunity by WBCs, clotting by platelets.
- Arteries: Carry blood away from heart (high pressure, thick wall).
- Veins: Return blood to heart (low pressure, thin wall).
- Capillaries: Exchange of gases and nutrients between blood and tissues.
- Blood groups: A, B, AB, O (based on antigens); +/- (Rh factor).

**Respiratory System:**
- Upper: Nose, trachea branch to bronchi.
- Lower: Bronchioles, alveoli (gas exchange).
- Diaphragm: Muscle controlling breathing (contracts = inhale, relaxes = exhale).
- Gas exchange: O2 in, CO2 out at alveoli.
- Breathing rate: 12-16 times per minute at rest.

**Digestive System:**
- Path: Mouth to Esophagus to Stomach to Small intestine to Large intestine to Rectum to Anus.
- Mouth: Teeth break food, saliva has amylase (carb digestion).
- Stomach: HCl acid, pepsin enzyme (protein digestion), churns 2-3 hours.
- Small intestine: 6-7 meters, main absorption site, pancreas secretes enzymes, liver secretes bile.
- Large intestine: Water absorption, forms feces.
- Enzymes: Amylase (carbs), Protease (proteins), Lipase (fats).

**Excretory System:**
- Kidneys: Filter blood, produce urine (remove urea, excess water and ions).
- Nephron: Functional unit; glomerulus (filtration), from Bowmans capsule.
- Ureter: Carries urine from kidney to bladder.
- Bladder: Stores urine; capacity ~500 mL.
- Urethra: Expels urine from body.

**Endocrine System:**
- Hormones: Chemical messengers regulating body functions.
- Pituitary gland: Master gland, secretes growth hormone, TSH, ACTH, FSH, LH.
- Thyroid: Secretes thyroxine (metabolism control).
- Pancreas: Secretes insulin (glucose control), glucagon.
- Adrenal: Adrenaline (stress), cortisol.
- Testis/Ovary: Sex hormones (testosterone, estrogen, progesterone).

### Vital Organs

**Brain:**
- Mass: ~1.4 kg; 100 billion neurons.
- Regions: Cerebrum (largest, thought), Cerebellum (coordination), Brainstem (vital functions).
- Cerebrospinal fluid: Protection, nutrient transport.

**Heart:**
- Mass: ~300 grams (fist-sized).
- Chambers: Right atrium/ventricle (deoxygenated blood), Left atrium/ventricle (oxygenated blood).
- Cardiac cycle: Atrial systole to Ventricular systole to Diastole (all relax).

**Lungs:**
- 2 lungs (left smaller due to heart).
- Surface area: ~70 m² (due to alveoli).
- Vital capacity: ~3.5 L in adults.

**Liver:**
- Largest internal organ (~1.5 kg).
- Functions: Detoxification, glycogen storage, protein synthesis, bile production.

**Kidneys:**
- 2 kidneys (~150 grams each).
- Filter ~180 L blood daily; produce ~1.5 L urine.
- Regulate blood pressure, electrolytes, pH.

**Pancreas:**
- Mixed gland: Exocrine (digestive enzymes) and Endocrine (insulin, glucagon).
- Insulin: Lowers blood glucose; Glucagon: Raises blood glucose.

### Common Epidemics and Diseases

**COVID-19:**
- Cause: SARS-CoV-2 virus.
- Transmission: Respiratory droplets, aerosol, contact.
- Symptoms: Fever, cough, fatigue, loss of taste/smell, pneumonia (severe).
- Prevention: Vaccination, mask wearing, social distancing, hand hygiene.

**Tuberculosis (TB):**
- Cause: Mycobacterium tuberculosis (bacterium).
- Transmission: Respiratory droplets, airborne.
- Symptoms: Persistent cough (>3 weeks), chest pain, hemoptysis, fever, night sweats.
- Prevention: BCG vaccination, TB screening, treatment.
- Treatment: 6-month drug regimen (isoniazid, rifampicin, ethambutol, pyrazinamide).

**Malaria:**
- Cause: Plasmodium parasites (P. falciparum, P. vivax, P. ovale, P. malariae).
- Transmission: Female Anopheles mosquito bite.
- Symptoms: Fever (periodic), chills, sweating, headache, muscle pain.
- Prevention: Mosquito avoidance, insecticide-treated nets, antimalarial drugs.

**Dengue:**
- Cause: Dengue virus (4 serotypes), transmitted by Aedes mosquito.
- Symptoms: High fever, rash, joint pain (dengue), bleeding (dengue hemorrhagic fever).
- Prevention: Mosquito control, dengue vaccine (in some regions).

**Cholera:**
- Cause: Vibrio cholerae bacterium.
- Transmission: Contaminated water, poor sanitation.
- Symptoms: Severe watery diarrhea (rice-water), vomiting, dehydration.
- Prevention: Safe water supply, sanitation, hand hygiene.
- Treatment: Oral rehydration solution (ORS), antibiotics, intravenous fluids.

**Typhoid:**
- Cause: Salmonella typhi bacterium.
- Transmission: Contaminated food/water.
- Symptoms: Sustained fever (39-40 Celsius), headache, weakness, abdominal pain.
- Prevention: Vaccination, safe water, food hygiene.

**Hepatitis:**
- Cause: Hepatitis viruses (A, B, C, D, E).
- Transmission: A & E (fecal-oral), B, C, D (blood contact, sexual).
- Symptoms: Jaundice, fatigue, abdominal pain, dark urine.
- Prevention: Vaccination (A, B), safe water (A, E), safe sex.

**AIDS:**
- Cause: HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus).
- Transmission: Sexual contact, blood, mother-to-child.
- Symptoms: CD4 count <200 cells/mm³; opportunistic infections.
- Prevention: Antiretroviral therapy (ART), safe sex, PrEP.

### First Aid

**Basic First Aid:**
- ABC rule: Airway, Breathing, Circulation.
- CPR: Chest compressions (100-120/min) plus rescue breaths.
- Recovery position: Unconscious but breathing, tilted head, drain fluids.
- Wound care: Stop bleeding (pressure), clean, dress, elevate if limb.
- Choking: Heimlich maneuver (abdominal thrusts above navel).
