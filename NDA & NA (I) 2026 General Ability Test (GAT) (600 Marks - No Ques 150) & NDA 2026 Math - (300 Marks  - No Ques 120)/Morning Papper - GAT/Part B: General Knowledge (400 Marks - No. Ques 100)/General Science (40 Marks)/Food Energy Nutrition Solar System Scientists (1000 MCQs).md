# NDA 2026 General Science: Food, Energy, Nutrition, Solar System & Scientists (1000 MCQs)

## Important Notes and Formulas

### Food as Source of Energy

**Energy Content:**
- Carbohydrates: 4 kcal/gram; primary energy source, glucose is main carb.
- Proteins: 4 kcal/gram; also amino acids, essential for repair and growth.
- Fats (Lipids): 9 kcal/gram; most concentrated energy source, stored in adipose tissue.
- Alcohol: 7 kcal/gram (not a nutrient, empty calories).

**Energy Pathways:**
- Cellular respiration: Glucose (C6H12O6) + O2 → CO2 + H2O + Energy (ATP).
- Anaerobic respiration: Glucose → Lactic acid + Energy (2 ATP per glucose).
- Aerobic respiration: More ATP produced (~30-32 ATP per glucose).

**Daily Energy Requirements:**
- Sedentary adult: 2000-2200 kcal/day.
- Moderate activity: 2200-2500 kcal/day.
- Heavy activity: 2500-3000+ kcal/day.
- Children: Higher per body weight; adolescents need more during growth.
- Pregnancy: +300 kcal/day; lactation: +500 kcal/day.

**Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR):**
- Energy needed for basic body functions at rest.
- Controlled by thyroid hormone (thyroxine).
- Factors: Age, sex, body composition, genetics.

### Balanced Diet

**Six Components of Balanced Diet:**

**1. Carbohydrates (45-65% of calories):**
- Simple: Glucose, fructose, sucrose (sweet taste, quick energy).
- Complex: Starch (potatoes, grains), fiber (vegetables, fruits).
- Functions: Energy, structural (cellulose), fiber for digestion.
- Sources: Grains, cereals, bread, rice, pasta, vegetables, fruits.

**2. Proteins (10-35% of calories, ~0.8 g/kg body weight):**
- Structure: Amino acids linked by peptide bonds; 20 amino acids (9 essential).
- Functions: Repair, growth, enzymes, hormones, immunity, hemoglobin.
- Animal sources: Meat, fish, eggs, milk, cheese (complete proteins, all 9 amino acids).
- Plant sources: Legumes, nuts, seeds (often incomplete, lack one amino acid).
- Complete protein combinations: Rice + beans, bread + peanut butter.

**3. Fats/Lipids (20-35% of calories):**
- Saturated: Animal fat, coconut oil (increase cholesterol, reduce with age).
- Unsaturated: Vegetable oils, nuts, fish (omega-3 fatty acids, heart-healthy).
- Functions: Energy, hormone production, vitamin absorption (A, D, E, K), insulation.
- Recommended: Limit saturated to <10% calories; increase unsaturated.

**4. Vitamins (Organic, necessary for metabolism):**
- Fat-soluble: A (vision), D (calcium), E (antioxidant), K (clotting).
- Water-soluble: B-complex (energy, neuroprotection), C (antioxidant, collagen).
- Deficiencies: A (night blindness), C (scurvy), D (rickets), B12 (pernicious anemia).

**5. Minerals (Inorganic, necessary for body functions):**
- Calcium: Bone, teeth, muscle contraction; milk, cheese, leafy greens.
- Iron: Hemoglobin, oxygen transport; red meat, spinach, legumes.
- Iodine: Thyroid hormone; iodized salt, seafood.
- Potassium: Nerve signaling, heart rhythm; bananas, potatoes, beans.
- Sodium: Fluid balance, nerve signaling; salt (limit to <2.3g/day).
- Zinc: Immunity, wound healing; oysters, beef, seeds.

**6. Water (60-70% of body weight):**
- Functions: Solvent, thermoregulation, nutrient transport, lubrication.
- Requirement: ~8 glasses/day; more with exercise or heat.
- Dehydration: Fatigue, reduced cognition, heat exhaustion.

**Balanced Diet Guidelines:**
- RDA (Recommended Dietary Allowance) varies by age, sex, activity.
- USDA MyPlate: Half vegetables/fruits, quarter grains, quarter protein.
- Mediterranean diet: High in vegetables, fruits, whole grains, olive oil, fish.
- Ideal ratio: 45-65% carbs, 10-35% protein, 20-35% fat.

### Solar System

**Components:**
- Sun: Central star; 99.86% of solar system mass.
- Planets: 8 (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune).
- Moons: Natural satellites orbiting planets.
- Asteroids: Rocky bodies between Mars and Jupiter.
- Comets: Icy bodies with tails from Kuiper Belt.
- Meteors: Meteoroids entering atmosphere (shooting stars).
- Meteorites: Meteors reaching Earth surface.

**Planets:**
- Inner (Terrestrial): Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars (small, rocky, no rings).
- Outer (Gas/Ice Giants): Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune (large, gaseous, rings).
- Earth: Distance from Sun 150 million km (1 AU), diameter 12,742 km, 1 moon, Goldilocks zone.

**Meteors:**
- Definition: Meteoroid entering atmosphere at high speed (11-18 km/s).
- Heating: Friction with atmosphere causes luminescence (shooting star).
- Meteorites: Fragments reaching Earth surface; types: Iron, Stone, Stony-iron.
- Significance: Water delivery to early Earth; extinct dino impact (65 million years ago).
- Meteor showers: Debris from comet orbits (Perseids Aug, Leonids Nov, Geminids Dec).

**Comets:**
- Definition: Icy bodies with rock/dust core; develop tails when near Sun.
- Structure: Nucleus (icy core), coma (gas cloud), tails (gas and dust).
- Famous: Halley's Comet (every 75-76 years), last 2061.
- Orbital period: Some short-period (Halley: 75 years), long-period (thousands of years).
- Origin: Kuiper Belt (icy bodies beyond Neptune), Oort Cloud (theoretical spherical shell).

**Eclipses:**
- Solar Eclipse: Moon passes between Sun and Earth, blocks sunlight.
  - Types: Total (day becomes night locally), Partial (moon covers part), Annular (moon too far, ring visible).
  - Duration: ~2-3 minutes for totality.
  - Frequency: 2-5 solar eclipses per year; total occurs ~375 years in one location.
  - Path: ~200 km wide "path of totality."

- Lunar Eclipse: Earth passes between Sun and Moon, blocks moonlight reaching Moon.
  - Types: Penumbral (Moon in Earth's penumbra, subtle), Umbral (partial or total).
  - Total lunar eclipse: Moon enters Earth's umbra (total shadow), appears red (Rayleigh scattering).
  - Duration: Up to 1.5 hours in totality.
  - Frequency: 0-3 lunar eclipses per year; less common than solar.

- Saros Cycle: 18 years 11.3 days; same eclipse repeats with similar characteristics.
- Nodes: Points where Moon orbital plane intersects Earth orbital plane; eclipses occur near nodes.

### Planet Details

**Mercury:** Smallest, closest to Sun, extreme temperatures (-170 to 430°C), no atmosphere.
**Venus:** Hottest planet (460°C), thick CO2 atmosphere, 243-day rotation (retrograde).
**Earth:** Perfect conditions, 1 moon, 24-hour rotation, 365.25-day orbit.
**Mars:** Red planet (iron oxide), 2 small moons, thin atmosphere, polar ice caps.
**Jupiter:** Largest planet, 63+ moons, Great Red Spot (storm), strong magnetic field.
**Saturn:** Second largest, prominent ring system, 62+ moons, low density.
**Uranus:** Ice giant, tilted 98° (retrograde rotation), faint rings, 27 moons.
**Neptune:** Farthest, strong winds, 14+ moons, Triton (retrograde moon).

### Eminent Scientists and Achievements

**Isaac Newton (1642-1727):**
- Laws of Motion: 1) Objects stay at rest/motion unless force applied. 2) F=ma. 3) Action-reaction equal.
- Law of Universal Gravitation: F = G(m1×m2)/r²; explained planetary motion, tides.
- Calculus: Mathematical foundation for physics.
- Optics: White light is mixture of colors; prism experiment.

**Albert Einstein (1879-1955):**
- Special Relativity (1905): E=mc²; time dilation, length contraction, mass-energy equivalence.
- General Relativity (1915): Gravity as spacetime curvature; explained Mercury's orbit, gravitational lensing.
- Photoelectric Effect: Light consists of photons; won Nobel Prize 1921.
- Impact: Revolutionized physics, predicted black holes, gravitational waves.

**Marie Curie (1867-1934):**
- Discovered Polonium and Radium; only person win Nobel Prize in two fields (Physics 1903, Chemistry 1911).
- Radioactivity: Coined term; distinction between rays and particles.
- Medical application: Portable radiography units ("petites Curies") for WWI hospitals.
- First woman to win Nobel, first female professor at University of Paris.

**Charles Darwin (1809-1882):**
- Evolution by Natural Selection: Life evolves over time through differential survival.
- "Origin of Species" (1859): Evidence from fossils, biogeography, artificial selection.
- Finches in Galapagos: Different beak sizes for food sources.
- Impact: Explained biodiversity without need for creation; unified biology.

**Galileo Galilei (1564-1642):**
- Telescope Observations: Jupiter's moons, Moon's craters, sunspots; proved Copernican model.
- Kinematics: Acceleration due to gravity (~9.8 m/s²), projectile motion.
- Pendulum: Period independent of amplitude (isochronism); used for timekeeping.
- "Father of Scientific Method": Emphasized observation and experimentation over authority.

**Stephen Hawking (1942-2018):**
- Black Hole Radiation: Hawking radiation (1974); black holes not completely black.
- Singularities: Worked with Roger Penrose on gravitational collapse.
- Cosmology: Big Bang singularity, no-boundary proposal with Jim Hartle.
- Communication: Used speech synthesizer; inspired millions despite ALS.

**Nikola Tesla (1856-1943):**
- Alternating Current (AC): Practical system; defeated Edison's DC in "War of Currents."
- Transformer: Stepped voltage up/down, enabling long-distance power distribution.
- Wireless Transmission: Experimented with wireless power; inspired future radio.
- Inventions: Induction motor, X-ray production, remote control concepts.

**Louis Pasteur (1822-1895):**
- Germ Theory: Microorganisms cause disease; disproved spontaneous generation.
- Vaccination: Developed rabies vaccine; first human receives 1885.
- Pasteurization: Heat treatment kills pathogens, preserves food.
- Fermentation: Understood microbial fermentation; improved beer, wine, silk production.
