# NDA 2026 Chemistry: Preparation, Properties, and Industrial Applications (1000 MCQs)

## Important Notes and Formulas

### Hydrogen
- Preparation: Water + hot iron: 3Fe + 4H2O → Fe3O4 + 4H2; Acid + metal: Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl2 + H2.
- Properties: Lightest element; colorless, odorless; supports combustion; burns with pop sound.
- Uses: Production of ammonia, synthetic fuels, welding, laboratories.
- Hydrogen combines with non-metals: 2H2 + O2 → 2H2O; N2 + 3H2 → 2NH3 (Haber process).

### Oxygen
- Preparation: Decomposition of H2O2: 2H2O2 → 2H2O + O2 (with MnO2 catalyst).
- Properties: Odorless, colorless gas; supports combustion; needed for respiration.
- Uses: Medical use, welding, combustion processes, production of oxides.
- All burning is rapid oxidation; slow oxidation is rusting and respiration.

### Nitrogen
- Preparation: Removal of oxygen and CO2 from air; Denitrification of ammonium salts.
- Properties: Colorless, odorless; inert; does not support combustion.
- Uses: Production of ammonia (Haber process), fertilizers, inert atmosphere.
- Nitrogen fixation: N2 + 3H2 → 2NH3 (industrial); Natural: by bacteria in soil.

### Carbon Dioxide
- Preparation: Combustion of C: C + O2 → CO2; Acid + carbonate: 2HCl + CaCO3 → CaCl2 + H2O + CO2.
- Properties: Colorless, odorless (no smell), heavier than air; dissolves in water.
- Uses: Carbonated beverages, fire extinguishers, horticulture (photosynthesis), dry ice.
- Dry ice sublimates at -78°C.

### Oxidation and Reduction
- Oxidation: Loss of electrons or addition of oxygen; increase in oxidation state.
- Reduction: Gain of electrons or removal of oxygen; decrease in oxidation state.
- Oxidizing agent: Substance that causes oxidation (itself reduced).
- Reducing agent: Substance that causes reduction (itself oxidized).
- Redox reaction: Simultaneous oxidation and reduction.
- Example: Zn + Cu2+ → Zn2+ + Cu (Zn oxidized, Cu2+ reduced).

### Acids, Bases, and Salts
- Acid: Substance that donates H+ ions in water; pH < 7.
- Base: Substance that donates OH- ions or accepts H+; pH > 7.
- Salt: Ionic compound formed from neutralization of acid and base.
- Neutralization: H+ + OH- → H2O.
- pH scale: 0-14; 7 is neutral.
- Strong acids: HCl, H2SO4, HNO3; Strong bases: NaOH, KOH.

### Carbon—Different Forms
- Diamond: Carbon atoms in tetrahedral arrangement; hardest known substance; good thermal conductor but poor electrical conductor.
- Graphite: Carbon atoms in layers; soft, black, good electrical conductor; used in pencils, electrodes.
- Coal: Impure carbon; used as fuel.
- Charcoal: Porous carbon; used as adsorbent, decolorizer.
- Fullerenes: Closed-cage structures like C60 (buckminsterfullerene).

### Fertilizers
- Natural fertilizers: Manure, compost, bone meal; provide nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium.
- Artificial fertilizers: Urea, ammonium nitrate, superphosphate, potash.
- NPK: Three essential nutrients for plants (nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium).
- Nitrogen fixation produces ammonia, converted to nitrates for plant uptake.

### Industrial Preparations
- Soap: Made from fat/oil + NaOH by saponification. Saponification: C3H5(OCOR)3 + 3NaOH → C3H5(OH)3 + 3RCOONa.
- Glass: Mixture of SiO2 (sand), Na2CO3 (soda), CaO (lime) heated to 1700°C.
- Ink: Mixture of dyes, solvents, and other additives.
- Paper: Made from cellulose fibers; wood processed mechanically or chemically.
- Cement: Mixture of calcium compounds; CaCO3 + clay + heat → Portland cement.
- Paints: Pigments (color) + binder (oil/resin) + solvent.
- Safety Matches: Red phosphorus on box side; white phosphorus + sulfur on match head.
- Gun-powder: Mixture of potassium nitrate (KNO3), charcoal (C), sulfur (S) in ratio 75:15:10.

### Atomic Structure
- Atom: Nucleus (protons and neutrons) surrounded by electrons.
- Proton: Positive charge = +1e; mass ≈ 1 amu.
- Neutron: No charge; mass ≈ 1 amu.
- Electron: Negative charge = -1e; mass ≈ 1/1836 amu.
- Atomic number Z: Number of protons (determines element).
- Mass number A: Protons + neutrons.
- Isotopes: Atoms of same element with different neutron numbers.

### Atomic Equivalent and Molecular Weights
- Atomic weight: Average mass of an atom relative to C-12 = 12 amu.
- Molecular weight: Sum of atomic weights of atoms in molecule.
- Equivalent weight: Molar mass / valency.
- Molar mass: Mass of one mole of substance in grams.
- Avogadro's number: 6.022 × 10^23 particles per mole.

### Valency
- Valency: Number of electrons lost, gained, or shared by an atom.
- Valency of H: 1; O: 2; C: 4; N: 3; Cl: 1; Na: 1; Ca: 2.
- Variable valency: Elements with more than one valency, e.g., Fe (2+, 3+), Cu (1+, 2+).
- Valence electrons: Electrons in outermost shell.
