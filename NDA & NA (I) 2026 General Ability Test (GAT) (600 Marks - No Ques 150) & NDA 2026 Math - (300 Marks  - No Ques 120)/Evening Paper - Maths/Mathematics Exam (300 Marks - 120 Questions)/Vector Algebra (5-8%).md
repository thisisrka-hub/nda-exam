# NDA 2026 Mathematics: Vector Algebra (5-8%)

## Important Notes and Formulas

### Vector Basics
- A vector is a quantity with magnitude and direction.
- A vector in 2D or 3D can be written as a_x i + a_y j + a_z k.
- A zero vector has magnitude zero.
- Scalar multiplication c a scales the vector by c.
- Vector addition a + b adds components.
- Position vector of (x,y,z) is xi + yj + zk.

### Magnitude and Direction
- Magnitude of a = sqrt(a_x^2 + a_y^2 + a_z^2).
- Direction cosines are cos alpha = a_x/|a|, cos beta = a_y/|a|, cos gamma = a_z/|a|.
- A unit vector has magnitude 1.
- Unit vector along a is a/|a|.
- Vectors are parallel when one is a scalar multiple of the other.
- Vectors are perpendicular when their dot product is zero.

### Scalar (Dot) Product
- Dot product a.b = a_x b_x + a_y b_y + a_z b_z.
- Also a.b = |a||b| cos theta, where theta is the angle between a and b.
- Projection of a on b is (a.b)/|b|.
- Scalar triple product a.(b x c) equals the volume of the parallelepiped.

### Vector (Cross) Product
- Cross product a x b is the determinant of [i j k; a_x a_y a_z; b_x b_y b_z].
- Components are (a_y b_z - a_z b_y)i - (a_x b_z - a_z b_x)j + (a_x b_y - a_y b_x)k.
- Magnitude |a x b| = |a| |b| sin theta.
- Direction of a x b is perpendicular to both a and b.
- Area of the parallelogram formed by a and b is |a x b|.
- Area of the triangle formed by a and b is |a x b|/2.

### Magnitude and Direction Concepts
- |a+b|^2 = |a|^2 + |b|^2 + 2 a.b.
- cos theta = (a.b)/(|a| |b|).
- Direction ratios are proportional to vector components.
- The line equation r = a + t b uses a direction vector b.
